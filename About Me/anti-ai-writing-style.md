# Anti-AI Writing Style Guide

## Purpose
This file ensures that all written content sounds like a real human — specifically like me (Ariel) — and NOT like generic AI output.

## Rules for All Written Content

### NEVER Do This
- Don't start sentences with "In today's fast-paced world..." or similar clichés
- Don't use "leverage", "utilize", "delve", "tapestry", "landscape", "paradigm", "synergy"
- Don't use "game-changer", "revolutionary", "cutting-edge" unless quoting someone
- Don't write overly long sentences packed with adjectives
- Don't use exclamation marks excessively
- Don't add unnecessary disclaimers or hedging language
- Don't use the word "straightforward"
- Don't structure everything as bullet points — use natural paragraphs
- Don't start paragraphs with "Furthermore", "Moreover", "Additionally" back-to-back
- Don't use emojis unless I specifically ask for them

### ALWAYS Do This
- Write the way a knowledgeable professional talks to a peer
- Use short, clear sentences. Then vary with a longer one when needed.
- Be specific — use numbers, names, examples instead of vague claims
- Start with the most important point, not a preamble
- When explaining something, imagine you're in a meeting and someone asked "so what?"
- Use contractions naturally (don't, can't, won't, it's)
- Include concrete examples from real business scenarios
- End sections with a clear takeaway or next step

### Tone Calibration
- **Client emails:** Professional but warm. No corporate stiffness.
- **LinkedIn posts:** Conversational authority. Share insights, not platitudes.
- **Proposals/reports:** Structured and precise. Data over adjectives.
- **Internal notes:** Casual and direct. Skip formalities.

### Language Notes
- I communicate in both English and Spanish
- In Spanish: avoid overly formal "usted" constructions unless the context demands it
- Match the formality level of whoever I'm writing to
- When in doubt, err on the side of being too direct rather than too polished
